Before You Dive In

This masterclass covers 10 free or low-cost lead generation strategies every wholesaler should know. Work through them one at a time, pick 2–3 to focus on, and build from there.

Pick Your Focus

Don't try all 10 at once. Master 2–3 strategies before adding more. Consistency beats variety.

Volume Wins

The wholesaler making 30–50 contacts per week almost always outperforms one who searches for the "perfect" strategy.

Daily Action

Small daily actions compound into deals. Set a daily minimum and protect it like a job interview.

Follow Up

Most deals are won on the 5th–8th follow-up. Most wholesalers quit after the first "no." Don't be most wholesalers.

The Biggest Lesson: Most wholesalers don't fail because they don't know where to find leads — they fail because they stop after talking to 5 sellers. The goal isn't to find the perfect lead source. The goal is to have enough conversations that motivated sellers naturally appear.

01
Reverse Driving for Dollars
Free Beginner 1–2 hrs/session High Volume

Find distressed properties online using Google Maps Street View instead of driving neighborhoods — same results, zero gas money.

Lead Quality75%
Ease of Start90%
Speed of Results60%
Motivation Level80%

What to look for

  • Overgrown grass / dead lawn
  • Peeling or faded paint
  • Boarded-up windows
  • Tarps on roofs
  • Broken fences
  • Junk or debris in yard
  • Vacant-looking homes

Step-by-step process

  1. Open Google Maps
  2. Search neighborhoods with older homes
  3. Enable Street View mode
  4. Identify distressed properties
  5. Record the address
  6. Look up owner info (PropStream, county records)
  7. Contact the owner
Pro Tip: Use PropStream or BatchLeads to skip-trace owner info directly from the address. Set a timer for 90-minute sessions to stay focused and avoid rabbit holes.
Common Mistake: Spending all day looking at Google Maps without actually recording and contacting owners. The goal is 20 addresses per session, not 200.
Goal: Find 20 distressed properties in one sitting
02
Zillow FSBO
Free Beginner 30–60 min/day Very High

FSBO sellers are already trying to sell without an agent — they're open to creative solutions by nature. This is one of the warmest free lead sources available.

Lead Quality85%
Ease of Start95%
Speed of Results75%
Motivation Level85%

Red flags = opportunity

  • Listed 30+ days with no sale
  • Multiple price reductions
  • Poor listing photos
  • Vacant-looking property
  • Very short listing description

How to find them

  1. Go to Zillow.com
  2. Click "For Sale" filter
  3. Select "For Sale By Owner"
  4. Sort by Days on Market
  5. Look for 30+ day listings
"Hi [Name], I saw your property on Zillow. Are you still looking to sell? I'm a cash buyer and I can move quickly."
Pro Tip: Call in the morning between 8–10am. FSBOs are often at home and more likely to answer. Keep your opener short — just get them talking.
Common Mistake: Pitching too soon. Your job on the first call is to listen and qualify, not to make an offer.
Goal: Contact 10 FSBOs daily
03
Expired Listings
Low Cost Intermediate 1 hr/day High Motivation

These sellers already raised their hand and said they want to sell — they just didn't get the outcome they expected. Frustration creates flexibility.

Lead Quality88%
Ease of Start65%
Speed of Results80%
Motivation Level90%

Why they're motivated

  • Already tried to sell — and failed
  • Frustrated with the traditional process
  • May be willing to negotiate price
  • Likely carrying costs on unsold home

Key questions to ask

  1. "Why do you think it didn't sell?"
  2. "Are you still interested in selling?"
  3. "Would you consider a cash offer?"
  4. "What's your ideal timeline?"
"Hi [Name], I noticed your home was recently listed and came off the market. I work with cash buyers in the area and I was wondering — is the property still available?"
Pro Tip: Pull expired listings daily from the MLS (ask an investor-friendly agent) or use PropStream. The fresher the expiration, the hotter the lead.
Common Mistake: Leading with price too early. Let the seller tell you what went wrong first — that's where the deal lives.
Goal: Call 10–20 expired listings daily
04
Facebook Marketplace
Free Beginner 30–45 min/day

Facebook Marketplace is packed with unmotivated sellers hiding in plain sight — and almost no wholesalers are working it consistently.

Search terms that work

  • House for sale
  • Handyman special
  • Investor special
  • Fixer upper
  • AS-IS sale

Red flags = opportunity

  • Poor or blurry photos
  • Very short listing description
  • Multiple price drops visible
  • Vacant-looking property
  • Price well below market
"Hi! I saw your listing on Marketplace. Is this still available? I'm a cash buyer and can close quickly — no agents, no fees."
Lead Volume90%
Ease of Start95%
Pro Tip: Check Marketplace every morning. New listings go up daily and the fastest responder usually wins the conversation.
Common Mistake: Writing long first messages. Keep it short — just confirm availability and express cash interest.
Goal: Message 20 sellers per day
05
Craigslist
Free Beginner 20–30 min/day

Old school, still effective. Craigslist sellers often skip the MLS entirely — which means less competition from other investors.

Sections to check

  • Real Estate For Sale
  • By Owner category
  • Investment Properties

Motivated language to spot

  • "Must sell"
  • "Cash only"
  • "Needs work / AS-IS"
  • "Price reduced"
  • "Owner motivated"
Lead Quality70%
Ease of Start95%
Pro Tip: You can also post your own "We Buy Houses" ads on Craigslist for free and let motivated sellers come to you.
Common Mistake: Only browsing listings once a week. Check daily — fresh posts disappear fast and motivated sellers move on.
Goal: 10 conversations daily
06
Investor-Friendly Realtors
Free Fastest for Beginners High Quality

Investor-friendly agents know the market, have access to pre-market listings, and actively want to send you deals — if you position yourself right.

Questions to ask agents

  1. "Any fixer uppers available?"
  2. "Any properties about to hit the market?"
  3. "Any listings struggling to sell?"
  4. "Do you work with investors?"

How to build the relationship

  • Be serious and pre-qualified
  • Close quickly when you commit
  • Refer buyers to them
  • Check in weekly — not daily
"Hi, I'm looking for distressed properties or homes needing repairs in [AREA]. Do you work with investors? I close fast and pay cash."
Lead Quality88%
Relationship Compound95%
Pro Tip: Find agents who already work with flippers and landlords. They already speak your language.
Goal: Build relationships with 5 investor-friendly agents
07
JV With Other Wholesalers
Free Beginner-Friendly Fast Deals

Most wholesalers are sitting on dead leads, out-of-market deals, and buyers they can't match. You can be the solution — and split the fee.

What wholesalers have available

  • Dead leads from old campaigns
  • Deals outside their market
  • Buyers they can't match to deals
  • Contracts they can't close alone

Conversation openers

  1. "What market do you work?"
  2. "What deals are you currently moving?"
  3. "Do you JV? I have [buyers/deals/leads]."
Pro Tip: Be specific about what you bring to the table — a buyer list, a specific market, or seller leads. Vague outreach gets ignored.
Common Mistake: Reaching out to JV without anything to offer. Know your value before the conversation.
Goal: Connect with 5 wholesalers weekly
08
Driving for Dollars
Gas Cost Beginner Hidden Gems

Old school, still one of the most effective. You'll find properties that haven't been listed anywhere — invisible to every other investor.

What to look for

  • Vacant homes
  • Overgrown lawns
  • Deferred maintenance
  • Code violation notices
  • Broken windows or boarded doors
  • Trash / junk piles

Tools you need

  • Smartphone (camera + notes)
  • DealMachine or driving app
  • Skip tracing service
  • Notebook as backup
Lead Uniqueness95%
Ease of Start85%
Pro Tip: Use DealMachine to photograph and instantly tag properties while driving. It stores the address and pulls owner data automatically.
Goal: Add 25 new addresses weekly
09
Vacant Property Lists
Low Cost Intermediate Highly Motivated

Vacant property owners are carrying a burden they often don't know how to solve. You're not interrupting — you're offering a solution.

Why they're motivated

  • Inheritance situations
  • Landlord fatigue
  • Relocation or job change
  • Ongoing maintenance costs
  • Liability from empty property

Questions to ask

  1. "Are you interested in selling?"
  2. "Is the property currently occupied?"
  3. "Have you considered a cash offer?"
  4. "What's your situation with the property?"
Pro Tip: Pull vacant lists from PropStream, ListSource, or your county assessor's office. Mail + call = better response rate than either alone.
Goal: Contact 25 owners weekly
10
Networking
Free Most Ignored Compounds Over Time

Most new wholesalers ignore networking — but referrals and relationships often close faster than any cold outreach strategy.

Where deals come from

  • Facebook investor groups
  • Local meetups & events
  • Discord communities (like this one)
  • Local REIAs
  • Referrals from other investors

Daily networking activity

  1. Comment on 10 posts in investor groups
  2. Start 5 direct conversations
  3. Add 3 new investor contacts
Long-term ROI100%
Ease of Start90%
Pro Tip: Give before you ask. Share a deal, a resource, or a tip before asking for something. People remember who showed up for them first.
Goal: Build your investor network consistently, every week
